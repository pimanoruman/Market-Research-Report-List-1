<p><p>The multi-touch sensing market is primarily divided into opaque and transparent types. Opaque multi-touch sensors are designed for devices with non-transparent surfaces, ideal for applications like kiosks and ATMs, where visibility through the screen is not required. In contrast, transparent multi-touch sensors are used in devices that require visibility, such as smartphones and tablets, allowing users to interact with content while still seeing the display. Both types enhance user experience through responsive touch detection.</p></p>
