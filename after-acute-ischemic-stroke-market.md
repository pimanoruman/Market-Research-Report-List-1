<p><p>After an acute ischemic stroke, three primary therapeutic markets emerge: antihypertensive therapeutics, antiplatelet therapeutics, and anticoagulants. Antihypertensive agents help manage elevated blood pressure, reducing the risk of further strokes. Antiplatelet therapeutics, such as aspirin, inhibit platelet aggregation, preventing clot formation. Anticoagulants, like warfarin or direct oral anticoagulants, target blood coagulation pathways to prevent new clots. Together, these treatments aim to improve patient outcomes, minimize complications, and reduce the incidence of recurrent strokes in affected individuals.</p></p>
