Agarose gel electrophoresis is widely used for separating larger biomolecules, offering simplicity and cost-effectiveness. In contrast, polyacrylamide gel electrophoresis provides higher resolution and is ideal for smaller proteins and nucleic acids, allowing detailed analyses. Both systems play crucial roles in research, diagnostics, and biotechnology, catering to diverse applications in molecular biology and biochemistry.</p></p>
