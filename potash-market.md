<p><p>The potash market comprises three main types: Potassium Chloride (KCl), Potassium Nitrate (KNO₃), and Potassium Sulfate (K₂SO₄). Potassium Chloride is the most widely used, primarily in fertilizers for crops like corn and wheat. Potassium Nitrate, a chlorine-free option, is favored in high-value crops and specialty fertilizers due to its dual nitrogen and potassium content. Potassium Sulfate, also chlorine-free, is ideal for chloride-sensitive crops like fruits and vegetables. Each type caters to specific agricultural needs, balancing nutrient requirements and crop sensitivities.</p></p>
