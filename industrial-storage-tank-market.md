<p><p>The industrial storage tank market is primarily divided into pressurized and non-pressurized tanks. Pressurized tanks are designed to store fluids under high pressure, making them suitable for gases and volatile liquids, ensuring safety and stability. In contrast, non-pressurized tanks operate at ambient conditions, ideal for storing liquids like water and chemicals without the risk of explosive vapor releases. Both types serve diverse industries, including oil and gas, chemicals, and food processing, addressing specific storage requirements and operational safety concerns.</p></p>
