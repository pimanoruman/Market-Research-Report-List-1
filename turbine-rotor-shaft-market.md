<p><p>The turbine rotor shaft market encompasses two primary manufacturing processes: the standard process and the hot isostatic pressing (HIP) process. The standard process typically involves traditional forging and machining techniques, producing reliable shafts with established characteristics. In contrast, the HIP process utilizes high temperature and pressure to eliminate porosity, resulting in enhanced material properties, such as improved strength and fatigue resistance. Both methods serve critical roles in producing turbine rotor shafts for various applications, including power generation and aerospace.</p></p>
