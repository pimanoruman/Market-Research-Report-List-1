<p><ul><li>Chemical Vapor Deposition(CVD)</li><li>Physical Vapor Deposition(PVD)</li><li>Molecular Beam Epitaxy(MBE)</li><li>Electrochemical Deposition(ECD)</li><li>Atomic Layer Deposition(ALD)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The semiconductor deposition market encompasses various methods for creating thin films on substrates. Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) uses gaseous reactants for uniform coatings, while Physical Vapor Deposition (PVD) deposits material via vaporization techniques. Molecular Beam Epitaxy (MBE) allows for precise layer growth through molecular beams. Electrochemical Deposition (ECD) utilizes electrochemical reactions for film formation, and Atomic Layer Deposition (ALD) offers atomic-level control for intricate, uniform films. Each method serves specialized applications across semiconductor fabrication.</p></p>
