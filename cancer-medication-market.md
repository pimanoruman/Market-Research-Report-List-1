<p><p>The cancer medication market comprises various types, each targeting specific mechanisms of tumor growth. Antimetabolites disrupt DNA/RNA synthesis, hindering cell division. Alkaloid drugs, derived from plants, interfere with microtubule function, preventing mitosis. Hormone therapies block cancer's hormone-driven growth by altering hormone levels. Targeted drugs aim at specific cellular pathways, minimizing damage to normal cells. Platinum-based drugs like cisplatin bind to DNA, causing cell death. The "Other" category includes treatments such as immunotherapies and monoclonal antibodies, broadening therapeutic options.</p></p>
